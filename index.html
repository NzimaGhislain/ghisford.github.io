Our farmers matter...here's proof!

Agriculture remains the major source of livelihood in Uganda. According to the Uganda National Household Survey (UNHS) 2016/17, the bigger proportion of the working population is engaged in agriculture, forestry and fishing (65%). Among the females in the working population, 70% are engaged in agriculture compared to 58% of the males. The Agricultural sector accounted for the largest share of employment (36%). The agriculture sector had a total contribution to GDP at current prices of 24.9 percent in the FY 2016/17 compared to 23.7 percent in FY 2015/16. The food crop sub sector registered the highest contribution within the agricultural sector of 13.6 percent in FY 2016/17, an increase from 12.1 percent in FY 2015/16

So what exactly is the problem?

The Ugandan economy heavily relies on agriculture and that makes the farmers the most important piece of the system. The saddest thing is how most of them are exploited to sell their produce at extremely low prices. This has made agriculture an unprofitable business venture for youth,who make up more than 70% of the population. This is extremely dangerous because everyone on the planet must eat. Moreover, the final consumers,our families and friends, buy these cheaply acquired food very expensively,sometimes at 5 times the price it was bought at from the farmer. As a result, our parents can not afford enough food for healthy nourishment for their children and themselves.As the saying goes, you are what you eat! it is therefore a global concern.
